About

My work focuses on quantum machine learning, especially the design and analysis of trainable, noise-aware, and hardware-conscious quantum neural networks. I am interested in understanding when quantum models provide meaningful benefits, how hybrid quantum-classical systems should be designed, and how near-term hardware constraints affect practical QML performance.

Research focus

Trainability and expressibility of quantum neural networks, barren plateau mitigation, noise-resilient QNN design, and hybrid quantum-classical learning systems.

Broader interests

Hardware-aware quantum algorithms, quantum architecture search, analytical quantum cost modeling, and QML applications in finance, healthcare, classification, and path planning.

Research

Selected research directions that connect quantum algorithm design, machine learning, and practical constraints of near-term quantum computing.

Trainability and expressibility in QNNs

Studying how circuit depth, width, and entanglement patterns affect expressibility and optimization landscapes in quantum and hybrid quantum neural networks.

Hybrid quantum-classical architectures

Evaluating how quantum layers interact with classical pre- and post-processing modules, and when hybrid designs can offer practical benefits over classical baselines.

Noise-resilient QML

Designing models and training strategies that remain effective under NISQ-era noise, including approaches where noise can be incorporated into the learning process.

Quantum optimization and applications

Exploring QML and quantum optimization for application domains such as finance, healthcare, transportation, and decision-making problems.
